Electricity is the appropriate energy for lighting, and can save a lot if we use high-performance lamps.


Conduct. Options to improve efficiency


Although not change any lamp, we have several options to improve the efficiency of lighting in our house:


1) The classic advice: turn off the light when leaving a room has become outdated. According to a study by the Center for Energy, Environment and Technology (CIEMAT) and the School of Telecommunications Engineering (ETSIT), the optimum time before turning off a fluorescent lamp energy saving is about 43 minutes, below that time emissions and the associated economic costs soar.


The aim of the research was to know if it is more convenient to turn off the light when leaving a room or if instead it is better to leave it on, the paper concludes that emissions and the economic cost are reduced, whether the lamp is running the minimum hours as possible if the number of switching on and off is reduced to a minimum. If the user wants to reduce energy consumption associated with the use of the lamp is recommended that the light goes on exit if it is not expected to return to that room before 43 minutes.


2) A good measure is to use low-power lamps directed at the exact point where we need light-like a desk instead of very powerful lamps from the ceiling to illuminate a room.


3) The decorative lamp with many bulbs we have in the ceiling of the room can and should come on when there visits and celebrations, but should remain off the rest of the time.


4) Even the darkest homes can use sunlight. Often, simply lift a blind or bring the chair to the window.


Ecological-DIY


Plan and build good lighting:

Not a haggard bulb on the ceiling and a waste of directional lamps: good lighting should be pleasant, comfortable and efficient. In the case of internal lighting, a way to achieve considerable energy savings it is to incorporate flow reduction systems, which allow to reduce by 50% the light level and therefore consumption, from the time that we set. The introduction of these systems, which can regulate at the same time a large number of points of light, can be purchased on the market, are home automation to control lighting and temperature facilities.


Automatismos para la iluminación

On the market a number of gadgets that allow automatic control of lighting. They may pose a certain economy of energy. They are sold in kits to install them yourself:

Presence detectors. They are able to turn off the light automatically when the room is empty of human presence. They work by a motion sensor connected to the lamp.

Ambient light sensors. These devices consist of a light detector connected to the lamp. Turn off the light automatically when the intensity of sunlight reaches a certain level. (Of course, also the light when natural light decreases, so should be able to be turned off manually). Some energy saving lamps are marketed with a built-in sensor so that it is not necessary to install it separately.



Equipment renewal


Proposed actions: replace conventional lamps with energy-efficient models


1) The first is to count the number of lamps we have at home, and evaluate their respective powers, which often go from 40 to 120 watts.


2) Then let us see what are lit longer (an average of more than two or three hours a day).


3) Probably we shall find that we have two or three lamps in this case. These are what we must replace, in principle, low consumption models.

4) already in the store where we usually buy the bulbs should investigate low-power models available. The usual box equivalence is as follows:


The economizer 9 watt replace the 40, the 13 to the 60, the 18 to 75 and 25 to 100. Some brands offer even more energy-saving equivalents, for example 5/25; 7/40; 11/60; 15/75; 20/100 and 23/120. With regard to the average life, duration of conventional lamps usually 1,000 hours, and 10,000 for saving. These equivalences are usually included in the carton of lamps.


The energy efficiency of these lamps must exceed 40 lumen / watt, and life be over 8,000 hours. The container lamps also carries a similar energy tag used for appliances. The low power are invariably Class A, while incandescent do not pass the class F or G.


5) Other products for lighting energy economizers are electronic transformers for fluorescent, allowing instant on, save up to 30% energy, eliminate stroboscopic effect (the annoying flicker) and extend the life of the tube. There are many more economizers models, based on different technologies, on which we can obtain information on the manufacturers home.


6) Other products are in line of intelligent lighting, adapted to the changing environment. We can acquire dimmers (can not be used with fluorescent lamps) or even motion detectors that turn off the light automatically when the last person leaves the room.

Stoves, radiators and air conditioners consume energy to maintain a comfortable temperature inside our homes. How to get maximum comfort with minimum energy consumption?


In principle, everything depends on the climate zone where you are: if you live in the province of Avila or Soria, it is likely that the number of days with need to heat the house closer to 200 a year. By contrast, in the provinces of Almeria, this number may be less than 40 -in the coastal area. We can also evaluate the number of days with cooling demand in summer. With this in mind, you can see what the situation is closer to your particular case.



Conduct. Regulate temperature

You have a unique stove or a small air conditioner or a complete central heating system, the key is to properly regulate the temperature. This can be done in several ways:


1) Decide the comfort temperature you need. Usually it estimated (in the winter) in 19 or 20 degrees a suitable temperature; above 23 or 24, you're probably wasting fuel and money. The proper temperature would be between 25 and 27 degrees Celsius in the summer. Do not forget that the thermostat down one degree can save between 5 and 8% of energy.


2) Decide the location: the room must be conditioned, but probably the bedroom or kitchen need not just heating.


3) Decide when you want climatizar: rather than keep the heat all day, it may suffice to turn it on only early in the morning and then the late afternoon. The same is true of the use of air conditioning. Difficulty: if heating is not adjustable.


4) Use with blinds and curtains ability to improve the insulation of the house. For example, during the hottest hours of blinds and curtains closed will prevent between the sweltering heat outside.


5) Ventilate the house the essentials: just a few minutes to renew the air of the house (sure, however, that the house has its regulatory outputs gases). In winter, open windows only long enough. Ten minutes are enough to completely renew the air in a room.


6) The plants are excellent air conditioners, especially in summer. The evaporated water helps cool the atmosphere.


7) Take advantage of the possibilities of regulating your stoves and radiators: many have timers, thermostats and temperature regulators fine.


8) Remember that good maintenance of the boiler increases performance.

9) Do not cover or put anything on the radiators, it hinders adequate diffusion of hot air.

10) Take advantage of blinds and awnings to protect the housing peak hours in summer sunshine. In the winter time do just the opposite, lets the sun's heat.


11) Close blinds and curtains at night to avoid losses.

Ecological-DIY

Location

1) Locate your moving parts heating or air conditioning in the most convenient location: it is best to place them in the center of the rooms, where they can cast their heat or cold in all directions. If placed near a window, for example, some of the heat or cold will escape through the glass.

2) Sometimes, it is effective to place an insulating and reflective foil behind radiators. It will not escape the heat to the wall and the street.

Place a thermostat

You install a small thermostat connected to it. It will make the system automatically turn off when the desired temperature is reached. Also they exist in the market timers then automatically connected the system: for example, we can regulate to turn on at 7 in the morning and to go out when we left home.

The theoretical performance of the installation can be 3 kWh of heat obtained for each kWh of electricity. It is considered, therefore, as a system much more economical and efficient heating than electric heating classic.

Good insulation minimizes air conditioning needs of the house. It also helps to reduce the impact of street noise inside the house. Here we offer insulation solutions for each case, from the simplest to the most complete.

Conduct


Evaluate the thermal performance of your home

1) If it is an interior floor, stuffed inside a building, it is likely that the thermal insulation is good (although perhaps light is scarce). By contrast, an attic can be very cold in winter and very hot in summer.

2) The old buildings built before 1900, usually have thick walls, reducing heat loss or cold.

3) The large glass surfaces facing the sun will warm us in winter, but they create a greenhouse effect in summer the temperature rise much.

Watch ventilation

1) Keep in mind that doors and windows are the weakest points of the house. The heat escapes quickly through the framework of a maladjusted, or through conventional glass windows of a few millimeters thick.

2) Of course, we must consider the need to ventilate the house. It is necessary to renew the air, but, usually, just a few minutes.

Ecological-DIY

1) The first is to make a list of weak points where heat escapes: especially door frames and windows. Some poorly fitting windows and doors leak lot of hot air in winter, and we will continue shivering even put the oven at full power.

2) Find out the neighborhood hardware store on supply of insulating weatherstripping. Purchase the amount needed and install them in the chosen points.

The most effective are the rubber weatherstrip, which are more expensive than foam. The first is to measure the amount of material needed and cut the appropriate width.

Then the strips are cut to the exact measurements and placed starting from a corner. For the protection is total, the strips facing in the fixed part of the window looking out and moving inward stick.

3) You can place a seal at the bottom of the door, as a rubber skirt or other material.

4) It may be a good idea to replace other thicker curtains. You can also install light shutters or blinds, especially useful to protect the house from summer heat. Some manufacturers sell reflective blinds, especially suitable for skylight windows facing the sun.

Conduct


The refrigerator allows us to have fresh food all year round.But also on 24 hours a day, 365 days a year. How can we take advantage of it while reducing energy consumption? Whatever our refrigerator model, we can do many things for their energy consumption is minimized.

Heat away

If the device is located in a very hot environment -pegado to a kitchen, for example heat transfer within the unit to the outside you will need more energy.

Close

Leave the door open longer than absolutely necessary cause a similar effect of energy wastage.

Unfreeze

If the model is not the type "frost-free" (no frost, so to speak), a thick layer of ice will form in a few months in the freezer. If not regularly defrosted the appliance, the engine will have to work to keep frozen forced several kilos of ice formed.

Wait

Something that almost everyone does, but it is worth remembering: should wait for cooked food to room temperature before placing in the fridge. Very hot foods forces the engine to work at full capacity to again reach the storage temperature.
